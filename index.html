Project Vision

The Future of Textbooks

Augmented Reality (AR) is fundamentally transforming modern education by introducing immersive, interactive learning experiences. Historically, students have relied on traditional e-books and physical texts that are confined to static, two-dimensional images and paragraphs. Our AR-based e-book shatters these limitations by seamlessly integrating interactive 3D models, active animations, and live scientific simulations directly onto the physical page.

By employing Marker-Based Tracking, the system recognizes specific printed images—such as a titration apparatus or the solar system—and superimposes dynamic, computer-generated graphics over them in real-time. This spatial computing approach allows learners to visualize complex, abstract scientific concepts (like orbital mechanics or chemical neutralization) with a level of depth and interactivity that a standard textbook simply cannot provide.

Crucially, this project was engineered using WebAR technology. This bridges the gap between physical materials and digital intelligence without requiring users to download heavy, dedicated applications or invest in expensive AR hardware. By simply scanning a target image with a standard smartphone camera, high-fidelity 3D assets are rendered directly in the mobile browser, making advanced educational technology highly accessible, scalable, and instantly deployable.

Simulation 01

Acid-Base Titration

Acid-Base titration is a fundamental quantitative chemical analysis used to determine the exact concentration of an acidic or basic solution.

In our simulation, observe a Strong Acid and Strong Base reaction. As Sodium Hydroxide (NaOH) drops into Hydrochloric Acid (HCl), the reaction pushes towards neutralization. At the Equivalence Point (pH 7.0), the indicator triggers a rapid chemical shift, turning the clear liquid vibrant pink.

Simulation 02

The Solar System

Our Solar System comprises a central star—the Sun—and all the celestial bodies gravitationally bound to its immense mass, including the inner rocky terrestrial planets and the massive outer gas giants.

Through this module, you can visualize the vast cosmic scale, differing rotational speeds, and complex elliptical paths of the planets directly in your physical space, allowing you to intuitively grasp the physics of gravity and orbital motion.
